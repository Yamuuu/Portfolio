About

Nice to meet you, I'm Yamu, a designer based in Toronto, Canada.

My design philosophy is empathy-based, putting users' needs first. I’m passionate about crafting functional, user-centered interfaces and continuously expanding my skills across various design disciplines to create better products. I see interface elements as puzzle pieces that need to be put together to meet user needs.

I’m deeply passionate about accessibility and committed to making my designs universally inclusive. Growing up with a visually impaired father, I witnessed firsthand the challenges differently-abled users face when interacting with technology. This experience instilled in me a strong drive to create interfaces that are not only functional but truly accessible. My goal is to make technology and design usable for everyone.

Born between the dawn of the internet and the age of the iPad kid, I grew up exploring the digital world, which sparked my curiosity about technology. This passion led me to pursue a postgraduate degree in Interaction Design, and I’m currently furthering my expertise with a postgraduate certification in Front-End Web Design at George Brown College.
